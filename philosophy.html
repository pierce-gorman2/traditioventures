Pillar One

The Man

Character before company

Before we study the business in depth, we look at the man behind it. Character comes first, but it does not replace disciplined evaluation of the business itself. And before any other question is asked, there is one that is non-negotiable: Is this man a committed, active member of a local church?

Not a man who identifies as a Christian in broad terms. Not a man who watches services online occasionally. A man who is planted - known by a local church, accountable to elders or pastors, and actively serving alongside other believers. This is our first filter, and it is the one we will not move on. A man who is unaccountable in his faith will eventually become unaccountable in his business. The local church is not incidental to how we evaluate a man - it is foundational.

A man of genuine character, rooted in a local church, carrying a real calling - that is where we begin. From there, we evaluate whether the company, model, and execution are strong enough to justify real partnership.

II

Pillar Two

The Calling

Vision before venture

We partner with men who can clearly articulate what God placed inside them. Not a pitch - a calling. There is a difference, and you can hear it in the first five minutes of a conversation. A pitch is constructed. A calling is discovered. A pitch can be abandoned when the market shifts. A calling cannot.

We are not interested in men who are building because it is a good market opportunity. We are interested in men who are building because they cannot imagine not building. That conviction is the fuel that carries a venture through the hard seasons.

When a man knows why he is building - not just what - he becomes unstoppable. That man is who we look for.

Pillar Three

The Build

Execution with partnership

Calling without execution is just a dream. We provide the capital, strategy, and active partnership that turns vision into a real, operating, growing business. We are not passive investors who read quarterly reports. We are in the room with you.

Our partners bring decades of real operational experience - building companies, hiring teams, opening markets, navigating hard seasons. We put all of that in service of every man we back.

We open doors. We sharpen strategy before it costs you. We tell you the hard truth when you need to hear it - because we are genuinely for you, not just for the return. That is what a real partnership looks like.

IV

Pillar Four

The Legacy

Generational thinking from day one

We structure every investment with generational thinking from day one. What decisions need to be made now so this business is still thriving in fifty years? What values need to be encoded in the culture so they outlast the founder? These are questions we ask at the start - not at the end.

Proverbs 13:22 is not aspirational for us. It is operational. We do not consider a business fully built until it is built to be passed down.

And because a legacy passed down should be fully owned by the man passing it, we build structured equity buy-back opportunities into every partnership from the start. As the business grows, we create a clear path for you to buy back our equity - so that when the time comes to hand what you built to the next generation, you hand them something that is entirely theirs. No outside investors. No strings. Just the business, the legacy, and your name on it - free and clear.

IV

What We Look For

The Six Things We Evaluate
Every Man By

We are selective. We are personal. We are looking for specific things in every man we consider partnering with.

First - Non-Negotiable

Active membership in a local church

This is our first and non-negotiable filter. We partner with men who are planted in a local church - known by their congregation, accountable to spiritual leadership, and actively serving alongside other believers. A man who is unaccountable in his faith will be unaccountable in his business.

A clearly defined God-given calling

You must be able to articulate why you are building this - not just what the market opportunity is. We want to hear the calling underneath the company.

A viable business with real market demand

Calling is necessary but not sufficient. The business must be able to work in the real world. We help you build rigorously, not just hopefully.

Coachability and humility

The man who thinks he has nothing left to learn is the man most likely to lose what he built. We partner with men who are hungry to grow and humble enough to be sharpened.

Commitment to generational thinking

You must genuinely care about what happens to this business after you are gone - not as an afterthought, but as a driving motivation from day one.

Willingness to operate in real partnership

We are not transactional. We are in this with you. That means real openness, real accountability, and a level of trust that goes deeper than a standard investor relationship.
